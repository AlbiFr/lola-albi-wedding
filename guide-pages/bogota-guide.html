What to See

  • Monserrate: For a breathtaking view of the city, take the cable car or funicular up Monserrate mountain. It's best to go on a clear day!
  • La Candelaria: Explore the historic heart of Bogotá with its colorful colonial architecture, cobblestone streets, and vibrant street art.
  • Gold Museum (Museo del Oro): Discover one of the world's most dazzling collections of pre-Hispanic gold artifacts.
  • Botero Museum: Enjoy the works of Colombia's most famous artist, Fernando Botero, known for his "voluminous" figures.

Bogotá Dining & Nightlife Guide

Emblematic Restaurants

  • Andrés Carne de Res A unique and festive restaurant experience. There's one in the city (La Candelaria) and the original, larger one in Chía (a short trip outside Bogotá).
  • Cumbia House A vibrant restaurant and live music venue co-founded by the famous Colombian singer Carlos Vives. Great food, cocktails, and a lively atmosphere.

Great Food

  • Harry Sasson Fine dining at its best, with a mix of Colombian and international flavors crafted by one of the country's most renowned chefs.
  • Verano A contemporary restaurant with a relaxed vibe, serving creative dishes made with local ingredients.
  • KO Asina Kitchen Modern Asian fusion with bold flavors and beautifully presented dishes.
  • Café Amarti Cozy café offering high-quality Colombian coffee, light bites, and desserts.
  • KOI Trendy spot for fresh, flavorful sushi and Asian-inspired dishes.

Market Adventure: Paloquemao

  • Paloquemao Market For the adventurous, visit this bustling market to try exotic fruits, fresh flowers, and traditional Colombian snacks. A colorful, lively experience!

Party & Drinks

  • Furia A lively club with great music and a fun, energetic atmosphere.
  • Afrika Rooftop Rooftop bar with amazing city views, perfect for cocktails and evening vibes.
  • Rooftop Corona Enjoy panoramic city views and a vibrant atmosphere, ideal for evening drinks with friends.
