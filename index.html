Gimnasio Moderno 2:30pm

The Capilla de los Apóstoles at Gimnasio Moderno in Bogotá is a stunning example of mid-20th-century modernist architecture. Designed by Juvenal Moya Cadena and inaugurated in 1956, the chapel features a Greek cross layout and delicate, overlapping concrete shells that create a sense of light and openness. Its interior glows with 1,477 vibrant stained-glass windows imported from France by Jean Barillet, depicting biblical scenes and earning it the nickname “the classroom of colours.” Located within the school grounds, the chapel offers a serene and inspiring setting, perfect for moments of reflection and celebration.

CLub Los Lagartos 4:30PM

Club Los Lagartos in Bogotá is a place where nature and tradition come together. Founded in 1937 by engineer David Gutiérrez Vanegas as Club Aguas Calientes, it began as a retreat featuring natural hot springs and has since grown into a distinguished social and sports club, with golf courses, tennis and squash courts, a nautical ski lake, a gym, and an Olympic-sized pool.

For Lorenza, Club Los Lagartos holds a special place in her heart, filled with childhood memories of laughter, gatherings, and time spent with family and friends. Hosting the reception here celebrates not only her past but also the joy of sharing this meaningful day with loved ones in a place that has always been part of her life.

The "city of eternal spring" can be unpredictable! Days are often pleasant, but evenings can get quite cool. We recommend bringing a jacket, coat, or shawl to stay comfortable as we celebrate into the night.

Colombia 101: Travel Tips

For our guests traveling from afar, here is some helpful information for your trip.

A Note on Security

A key local saying is "No dar papaya". It means don't make yourself an easy target. Be aware of your surroundings and take care of your personal belongings.

More Info

Getting Around

Public transportation can be inefficient for visitors. We highly recommend using ride-sharing apps like Uber, DiDi, or Cabify for a safe and convenient way to navigate the city.

Cash is King

While cards are widely accepted, many smaller shops and taxis operate on a cash-only basis. It's a good idea to have some Colombian Pesos (COP) on hand for daily expenses.

Stay Connected

To avoid high roaming charges, consider getting a local SIM card upon arrival or using an eSIM service like Airalo before you travel. This will give you affordable data to navigate and stay in touch.

No Dar Papaya

This means don't make yourself an easy target for opportunistic crime. It is about being smart and aware, not fearful. Here are some practical tips:

    These are common-sense tips, and following them is simple! They'll help you enjoy a safe and wonderful experience.

  • Be Aware: Always be conscious of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas like markets, bus stations, or tourist spots.
  • Personal Belongings: Keep your bag zipped and in front of you. Don't hang it on the back of a chair in a restaurant.
  • Valuables: Keep valuables like phones, cameras, and passports out of sight when not in use. Avoid using your phone while walking on the street if possible.
  • Jewelry: Avoid wearing flashy or expensive-looking jewelry that might attract unwanted attention.
  • Taxis: Use reputable ride-sharing apps (Uber, DiDi, Cabify) instead of hailing taxis on the street, especially at night.
  • Walking at Night: Stick to well-lit, busy streets. If you're unsure about an area, it's better to take a taxi.

By following these simple, common-sense tips, you can ensure a safe and wonderful experience!
